Second Law: The Law of Acceleration

"The acceleration of an object depends on the force applied and the mass of the object."

What does this mean? If you push a shopping cart lightly, it moves slowly. If you push it harder, it moves faster. But if the cart is full (more mass), the same push will make it move slower.

Key Idea: The more force you apply, the faster an object accelerates, but heavier objects need more force to move.
