First Law: The Law of Inertia

"An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an external force."

Imagine a soccer ball on the field. It won't move unless you kick it. Similarly, if you roll it, the ball would continue rolling forever if there were no friction or other forces to stop it.

Key Idea: Objects resist changes to their state of motion. This resistance is called inertia.

Real-World Applications

Newton's First Law explains why seatbelts are essential in vehicles. When a car suddenly stops, passengers continue moving forward due to inertia until acted upon by an external force (like a seatbelt).
